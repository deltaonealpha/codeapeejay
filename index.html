    Keynote

    Domain: OPEN

    Teams allowed per school: 1

    No. Of Participants: 5 (Web Designing – 2; Digital Imaging – 1; Presentation - 2)

    Time Allotted: 6 mins + Q&A

    Last date of submissions: 22/11/2020 2359 Hours

    Round 1: Elimination: Top 10 teams proceed ahead

    Round 2: Finals : Three teams emerge victorious

    Topic: Hardware in smartphones has reached a stagnation point. Be it the Qualcomm Snapdragon 865+, or the Apple A14 SoC, hardware has far transcended software. Even most games today cannot leverage all this computation power! Hence, the next decade will be focused solely on software, and making it able to use the hardware to its full capacity.

    Execution: Participants must create a presentation on their concept of such software. NO WORKING SOFTWARE PROTOTYPE IS REQUIRED. It must be an investor-pitch-style presentation where only the idea is presented. A product render/ UI-design must be created for the Digital Imaging section. Final and software files both must be submitted (EX: .psd and image for photoshop) file must be submitted. Participants may create marketing samples for bonus points. For the Web Designing segment, participants must design a webpage for their product. Elements from Digital Imaging may be included here but DI will still be individually judged.


    Rules:

  • The judge's decision(s) will be final, binding, and unarguable.
  • Use of templates and closed-source assets is forbidden and will lead to disqualification.
  • Presentations must be openable on Microsoft Office.


    • Judgment Criteria:

    Presentation: (15 points)

    Creativity/ Design/ Quality/ Problem Solving/ Originality & Demonstration skills

    Digital Imaging: (15 points)

    Originality/ Ease of using the UI for the common person

    Web Designing: (15 points)

    Responsiveness/ Creativity/ Originality/ User Experience

    BONUS (5 points): Marketing material in the Digital Imaging segment

    Submissions: Participants must upload a .zip file including the website's source code, the presentation, and DI files (including all assets) on our site (https://code2020.co/submissions.html/) by November 20, 2020 2359 hours.
